Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme (CCFS) 2026: A One-Time Relief for Defaulting Companies

February 24, 2026 6 min read Compliance Alerts By Compliance Desk

The Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) has introduced the Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme, 2026 (CCFS-2026) through General Circular No. 01/2026. The Scheme provides a significant one-time opportunity for companies that have defaulted in filing annual returns and financial statements to regularize their compliance at a substantially reduced cost.

This initiative is expected to benefit thousands of companies, particularly MSMEs, private limited companies, OPCs, and inactive entities, by reducing the financial burden of accumulated additional filing fees while improving the accuracy of the MCA registry.

Background

Under the Companies Act, 2013, every company is required to file:

  • Annual Financial Statements (Section 137)
  • Annual Return (Section 92)

Since 1 July 2018, delayed filing of these forms attracts an additional fee of ₹100 per day, with no upper limit. For companies that have not filed annual documents for several years, the accumulated additional fees often become substantial.

Recognizing the hardship faced by businesses, MCA has introduced CCFS-2026, offering a limited-period compliance window.

Objectives of CCFS-2026

The Scheme aims to:

  • Enable companies to complete pending annual filings.
  • Reduce the burden of excessive additional filing fees.
  • Improve statutory compliance.
  • Update the MCA corporate registry.
  • Encourage inactive companies to either become dormant or voluntarily strike off.

Duration of the Scheme

Particulars Date
Scheme Opens 15 April 2026
Scheme Closes 15 July 2026

No extension has been announced. Companies should utilize this window to complete pending compliances.

Benefits Available Under CCFS-2026

The Scheme provides three options for eligible companies.

1. Complete Pending Annual Filings

Companies can file all pending Annual Returns and Financial Statements by paying:

  • Normal filing fees, plus
  • Only 10% of the applicable additional filing fees
Example

Suppose:

  • Normal filing fee = ₹600
  • Additional fee due = ₹1,00,000

Without the Scheme: Total payable = ₹1,00,600

Under CCFS:

  • Normal fee = ₹600
  • Additional fee = ₹10,000 (10%)

Total payable = ₹10,600

This represents a saving of 90% of the additional fees.

2. Apply for Dormant Status

Inactive companies may choose to become Dormant Companies under Section 455 by filing Form MSC-1.

Benefit:

  • Only 50% of the normal filing fee is payable.

Dormant status is useful where promoters intend to retain the company for future business but currently have no significant operations.

3. Strike Off the Company

Companies that no longer intend to continue operations may apply for voluntary closure by filing Form STK-2.

Benefit:

  • Only 25% of the prescribed filing fee is payable.

This enables inactive companies to exit the corporate registry at a much lower cost.

Companies Eligible Under the Scheme

The Scheme is applicable to all companies except the following:

  • Companies against which a final notice for strike-off under Section 248 has already been issued.
  • Companies that have already applied for strike-off.
  • Companies that obtained dormant status before commencement of the Scheme.
  • Companies dissolved through amalgamation.
  • Vanishing companies.

Immunity from Penalties

One of the most significant features of CCFS-2026 is the relief from penalties in specified situations.

Annual Return (Section 92) and Financial Statements (Section 137)

No penalty will be levied where filings are completed:

  • Before issuance of the adjudication notice; or
  • Within 30 days from the date of issuance of such notice.

However, if:

  • More than 30 days have elapsed after the notice, or
  • An adjudication order has already been passed,

then the Scheme does not waive the penalties already attracted. It only reduces the filing fees.

Other Forms

For forms such as ADT-1, FC-3, FC-4, Form 20B, Form 21A, Form 23AC, Form 23ACA, Form 23AC-XBRL, Form 23ACA-XBRL, Form 66, and Form 23B, immunity against future penal action is available provided:

  • The forms are filed during the Scheme period, and
  • No prosecution or adjudication proceedings had commenced before filing.

Consequences of Not Availing the Scheme

The MCA has made it clear that once the Scheme concludes on 15 July 2026, Registrars of Companies will initiate necessary action against defaulting companies under the Companies Act, 2013.

Companies that continue to remain non-compliant may face:

  • Continued accrual of additional filing fees.
  • Adjudication proceedings.
  • Monetary penalties on the company and its officers.
  • Potential strike-off proceedings.
  • Other regulatory actions under the Act.

Key Takeaways

  • CCFS-2026 is a one-time compliance opportunity for defaulting companies.
  • Companies can regularize pending annual filings by paying only 10% of the applicable additional filing fees.
  • Inactive entities can opt for Dormant Status at half the normal fee or Strike Off at only 25% of the prescribed filing fee.
  • The Scheme is available from 15 April 2026 to 15 July 2026.
  • Timely action during the Scheme may also provide immunity from penalties in eligible cases.
  • Companies should review their compliance status immediately and utilize this limited window to avoid future regulatory consequences.

Conclusion

The Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme, 2026 is one of the most significant compliance relief measures introduced by the MCA in recent years. By substantially reducing additional filing fees and providing a simplified pathway for dormant status or voluntary strike-off, the Scheme encourages companies to restore statutory compliance while maintaining an accurate and updated corporate registry.

For companies with pending MCA filings, this is a valuable opportunity to regularize defaults at a fraction of the usual cost. Promoters and professionals should review pending compliances well before 15 July 2026 to maximize the benefits available under the Scheme and avoid future enforcement action.
